## **Abstract**

The Plasma‑Resonance Metric Engine (PRME) is a speculative propulsion architecture based on the controlled interaction between resonant plasma structures and natural spacetime micro‑perturbations. Unlike classical warp concepts requiring exotic matter or negative energy, the PRME explores whether a carefully engineered plasma configuration can couple to and amplify naturally occurring metric fluctuations through long‑term resonance.

The system centers on a five‑strand plasma braid forming a toroidal “lens” around a protected interior region. Three strands consist of self‑confining plasma that maintains structural integrity and magnetic containment. Two counter‑rotating, high‑density “near‑matter” strands generate asymmetric energy distributions that may induce directional curvature gradients. By phase‑locking the system to ambient spacetime ripples and feeding energy into the resonance cycle over extended durations, the PRME aims to gradually build a macroscopic curvature wave capable of producing effective faster‑than‑light (FTL) travel without local superluminal motion.

This document outlines the conceptual structure, physical rationale, limitations, and open research questions associated with the PRME framework. It is released under the AGPLv3 license to ensure that all derivative work remains permanently open and accessible to the global scientific community.

## **1. Introduction**

Classical propulsion systems are fundamentally limited by reaction mass and energy scaling. Warp‑metric concepts offer theoretical pathways to effective FTL travel but require exotic matter distributions not known to exist.

The PRME proposes an alternative approach:  
**Instead of forcing spacetime into a desired configuration, the PRME attempts to resonate with naturally occurring metric fluctuations and amplify them over time.**

This approach is motivated by three observations:

- Spacetime exhibits persistent micro‑ripples from quantum and gravitational sources.  
- Resonant systems can accumulate energy gradually and efficiently.  
- Plasma structures can generate complex, tunable stress–energy distributions.

The PRME is not presented as a proven technology but as a structured theoretical exploration.
